Rhinoceros

Information

A rhinoceros , commonly abbreviated to 'rhino', is one of any five extant species of odd-toed ungulates in the family Rhinocerotidae, as well as any of the numerous extinct species. Two of the extant species are native to Africa and three to Southern Asia.

  • Black rhinoceros

    • The name "black rhinoceros" was chosen to distinguish this species from the white rhinoceros .

  • Indian rhinoceros

    • The Indian rhinoceros, or greater one-horned rhinoceros, has a single horn 20 to 60 cm long. It is nearly as large as the African white rhino

  • Javan rhinoceros

    • The Javan rhinoceros is one of the most endangered large mammals in the world. According to 2015 estimates, only about 60 remain, in Java, Indonesia, all in the wild. It is also the least known rhino species. Like the closely related, and larger, Indian rhinoceros, the Javan rhino has a single horn. Its hairless, hazy gray skin falls into folds into the shoulder, back, and rump, giving it an armored appearance.
